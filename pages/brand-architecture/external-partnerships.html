External partnerships

When can an external partner logo be co-branded?

There are two situations when HCA Healthcare would co-brand with an external partner. The first is when a formal partnership has been formed with the external entity and the external entity has control of the co-branding hierarchy (e.g., co-branding with Apple). In that scenario, we defer to their guidelines.

The second is a formal partnership with an external entity and HCA Healthcare has control of the co-branding hierarchy and thus is the priority brand. HCA Healthcare does not co-brand with our partners — therefore our logo should never appear with a partner's logo.

Lock-up with external partner logo

Rules for external partnership co-branding where HCA Healthcare is the priority brand

  • HCA Healthcare should always be to the left when locked up with a partner logo.
  • Partner logo must not exceed the size of the
    HCA Healthcare logo.
  • Partner logo must be vertically aligned with the
    HCA Healthcare logo.
  • There must be a clear divider separating both logos.
  • Always use the stacked version of the HCA Healthcare logo.

As mentioned, we will not always have complete control in co-branding with external partners, but please request that the HCA Healthcare logo be on the left, equal in size to the partner’s logo and vertically aligned whenever possible. This will work toward consistency across external partnership materials.

Our partners with actively signed contracts may use the HCA Healthcare logo with approval from the Brand Team. Reference to HCA Healthcare in text must be approved by the Communications and Brand Teams.

Preferred horizontal lock-up

This is the preferred usage for co-branding lock-ups.

When a partner logo is used, the preferred scaling is to match the height of the HCA Healthcare logo.

If a partner logo feels out of balance with the
HCA Healthcare logo when the height is matched, set the wordmark of the partner logo to 75% the height of the letter H in the HCA Healthcare logo.

A vertical HCA Healthcare Chrome Light divider should be used on all preferred horizontal lock-ups.

Alternate vertical lock-up

This alternate lock-up may be used when space limitations prevent the usage of the horizontal lock-up.

When a partner logo is used, the preferred scaling is to match the width of the HCA Healthcare logo.

If a partner logo feels out of balance with the
HCA Healthcare logo when the width is matched, set the wordmark of the partner logo to 50% the height of the letter H in the HCA Healthcare logo.

A horizontal HCA Healthcare Chrome Light divider should be used on all alternate vertical lock-ups.

There may be constraints when scaling a partner logo with the HCA Healthcare logos, so please use your best judgment and reach out to the Brand Team with any concerns.
