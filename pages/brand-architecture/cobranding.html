Co-branding

Due to our scale, reach and impact, we work with and exist alongside formal partner brands. This means there are times when the HCA Healthcare logo will need to be shown together with other logos, co-branding the asset. Careful thought must be taken to ensure that we are not misrepresenting either brand when co-branding.

When co-branding with our market brands, you should give careful consideration to the audience with whom you need to communicate.

Example of consumer- or patient-facing communications

When communicating to consumers or patients

The market brand should always be the only logo shown. The HCA Healthcare logo should not be used in consumer or patient materials.

Example of colleague and physician-facing communications — market/facility specific content

When communicating to physicians

When communicating to physicians the HCA Healthcare enterprise logo should always be present. When an asset contains market specific messaging it can be co-branded with the enterprise and market brand logo.

Example of investor- or payer-facing communications

When communicating to investors or payers on behalf of the enterprise

The HCA Healthcare logo should be used.

Example of colleague and physician-facing communications — whole enterprise

The HCA Healthcare logo and a market brand logo may be used on diagonal corners of a page

When communicating to colleagues

The HCA Healthcare design system must be used to ensure a consistent look and feel across materials.

The HCA Healthcare logo should always be present on the page. This will also allow colleagues to better understand the role and support provided by
HCA Healthcare.

Market brand and facility logos should not be used for communications containing enterprise messaging, with the exception of joint venture brands. Joint venture brands may co-brand with HCA Healthcare on enterprise messaging.

If the facility already uses HCA Healthcare or the Diamond Plus mark in its name and logo, then co-branding is not necessary. The market brand logo or facility logo should never be locked up with the HCA Healthcare logo, and must exist in diagonal corners of a page.

The market brand logo or the facility logo should never be larger than the HCA Healthcare logo.

The market brand logo or facility logo may only appear once per document. The enterprise logo may appear more than once per document.

Any facility contact information is shown in the HCA Healthcare typography. Please see the typography section for more information.

When communicating to potential colleagues

The HCA Healthcare logo should always appear on recruitment materials. Market brands or facility logos should not typically be used.
