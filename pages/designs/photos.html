Our photography

Photography is one of our most widely used and influential brand assets. All photos — whether they be stock or custom — must meet our brand style and standards.

They should persuasively convey their message while representing the diversity of both our patients and our colleagues. Diversity in photography means providing imagery that reflects the variety of characteristics that shape an individual person. We take care to show people across the broad spectrum of different ethnicities, ages, disabilities, sexualities and gender identities.

Photography should be engaging and personal, with an emphasis on lifestyle imagery that reflects our commitment to the whole health of the individual.

For custom photoshoots, please refer to the custom photoshoot guidelines for additional information regarding compliance and procedural guidelines.

Note: Please see the digital design guidelines for specific information on using photography for website communications.

Lifestyle photography

Images should show real life experiences, motion, movement and energy. They should evoke emotion and be thought-provoking. Be sure images work to support the copy and message. Images should have a clear and simple focal point. They should also be warm, authentic and driven by story.

Images should be engaging, with subjects displayed in interesting ways, such as from an over-the-shoulder perspective. Images may not include added graphical elements.

Clinical photography

Clinical photographs should focus less on the technical elements of healthcare and more on the human interactions. They also should be warm, authentic, driven by story and filled with emotion and meaning.

Whenever possible, consult with a clinical colleague or subject matter expert to ensure the clinical photography you are using is technically accurate and complements the content it is paired with.

Clinical photography guidelines

When using clinical images, never include medically inappropriate elements such as:

  • Stethoscopes around the neck
  • Long painted fingernails
  • Visible jewelry

COVID-19 considerations

  • No masks around necks
  • Show proper social distancing
