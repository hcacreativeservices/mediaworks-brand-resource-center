The Why and The How

Why did we build a new, purpose-driven brand?

The fact is, HCA Healthcare is doing incredible things. The new branding will help us uniformly connect to and convey our purpose and our story – how we show up for work each day, what we collectively stand for and, importantly, how we want patients to feel when they set foot in one of our facilities.

How is this different from what we have been doing?

This is a change in the way we communicate. It is how we can be consistent across the organization, to current colleagues and candidates, about what makes HCA Healthcare unique and about our opportunities based on our scale. We are bigger and more complex than when we were founded in 1968, and this will help us better engage colleagues and candidates, attracting them and reinforcing the opportunities they have working here.

Isn’t a brand just a logo?

Our brand is much more than just a logo. Our brand is made up of our combined efforts, interactions and communications. Our new logo, fonts, colors, visual elements, word choices and messaging all work together to help us represent and communicate our brand.

How will this affect our facilities?

We are not going to brand every facility as HCA Healthcare because we have great local brands in many communities already. But in areas where we have no brand to tie our access points together, this limits our ability to market ourselves as a system solution for patients, nurses and physicians. This is especially important to private insurers as they build their networks.

So, we’re exploring the use of HCA Healthcare as the umbrella brand in some of our communities where there is no market brand. That work has started in Houston and Florida, and subsequent areas will be addressed in the coming years via a phased approach.

But we want to be clear: defining and evolving our market brands is going to take considerable coordination among our teams. It’s a multi-phase effort that will have both short-term and long-term goals, so our approach is one of close coordination to ensure that we are all in sync moving along the same path.

Will this foster greater collaboration across our organization?

Yes. When we unite under one purpose, we can collaborate to raise the bar in healthcare. Care delivered in one location can change care delivered everywhere.

How will this affect my day-to-day?

For many of you, it won’t change a thing. The fact is, it doesn’t matter whether one of our facilities carries the HCA Healthcare logo. We’re all part of the same family, committed to providing the highest quality care our patients need and deserve.

I don’t work directly with patients in my job. Is the brand relevant for me too?

Yes, this is for everyone in every job within HCA Healthcare. No matter what you do, our brand purpose and promise connect all of us as one organization.

Brand Governance/Brand Stewardship

What is brand governance?

Brand governance is a formalized review process through which we can ensure consistent design and messaging across the enterprise.

What is different about HCA Healthcare’s approach to brand governance?

We are not the “logo police.” Our governance model is a stewardship model where our hope is to educate, support and empower colleagues as we all work towards being good stewards of the brand.

What is brand stewardship?

Stewardship is the activity or job of being responsible for something. In our case, that something is our brand. Through brand stewardship, our hope is to educate, support and empower colleagues as we all work towards being good stewards of the brand.

Lexicon

Do we have to abide by the lexicon guidelines?

Yes. Our word choices and writing style convey a message about who we are and what we stand for, and when we communicate on behalf of HCA Healthcare, we want to ensure that we speak with a unified, consistent voice.

How should we communicate the importance of understanding and following the lexicon guidelines to our colleagues?

Wherever you work or whatever your role, you are a part of HCA Healthcare. These guidelines will help all of us uniformly convey our purpose and our story, and communicate with one voice.

Can I use HCA on second reference?

No. Please use HCA Healthcare on every reference when talking about our organization.

Can I abbreviate HCA Healthcare to HCAH?

No. When referring to the enterprise always use HCA Healthcare.

What is the difference between the HCA Healthcare enterprise brand and the HCA Healthcare market brands?

The HCA Healthcare enterprise brand is the overarching brand for HCA Healthcare as a whole, across our more than 2,000 sites of care, office locations, (etc.).

Our HCA Healthcare market brands are regional consumer-facing brands—for example, HealthONE in the Denver area or HCA Houston Healthcare in Houston.

Who, specifically, does the HCA Healthcare enterprise brand speak to?

We use the enterprise brand to speak to colleagues, candidates, physicians and other healthcare industry stakeholders (e.g., payers, investors, legislators) to communicate who HCA Healthcare is and the value we deliver.

I’m trying to abide by the lexicon guidelines but the word colleague doesn’t really fit in my message—the word employee makes more sense. What do I do?

While there are words like colleague that we strive to incorporate into our communications, they are to be applied within reason. Always consider the audience and use the words that will best serve the situation.

Do I have to include a date in the text, or footnote, for each statistic or fact I use?

Due to the scale of our organization, statistics are always being updated on a frequent basis. Any number referenced in text should include an associated qualifying date (e.g., In 2018, HCA Healthcare donated $42 million to charitable organizations). One date stamp, under the text or in the footer, can also be used for communications with multiple referenced statistics (e.g., Note: All information current as of Month Year).

How do I learn more about AP style?

The Associated Press Stylebook is a writing style guide that outlines rules on grammar, punctuation usage and journalistic style. You can access the stylebook in print or online at apstylebook.com.

Naming

Can I create my own name for a program or event?

If you have a new program, event, facility or team that you are trying to name, contact Ask Brand for guidance. They can help you ensure your new name doesn’t infringe on anyone else’s trademark and is in compliance with our brand architecture guidelines.

Always take into account whether your program or event falls under the functional, flexible or free design style. Seasonal initiatives and one-off campaigns may allow for additional creative freedom and should be approved by the Brand Team.

Brand Design System

How do I know which design style to use—functional, flexible or free?

The baseline for all projects and materials is our functional design style. These layouts and designs are pre-existing and approved for all materials used regularly and printed repeatedly throughout the year. Examples include routine HCA Healthcare communications, training materials and evergreen collateral.

The flexible design style exists to build on functional styles and allows for customization while staying aligned with our brand. Creative Services and the Brand Team will approve flexible design style elements.

Working within the free design style is reserved for limited, one-off campaigns and requires Brand Team approval.

I’ve seen documents, collateral or design schemes that don’t look like they follow our brand standards. Are other departments allowed to act outside of the brand guidelines?

No, our brand standards are enterprisewide. Some projects fall under the flexible or free design styles and may have received approval for design variations from our Brand Team. If you see HCA Healthcare materials or designs that are counter to our brand standards and this Brand Book, follow the rule of if you see something, say something. Let our Brand Team know to ensure we have an opportunity to correct the issue.

Logo

May I use past HCA Healthcare logos?

You may not. All HCA Healthcare collateral must use the current HCA Healthcare logo.

May I add my internal program, group, division, service line or department name to the logo?

No, however, all approved logo lock-ups are available on the Brand Resource Center, located at HCAhealthcare.com/brand.

Can I add a logo to my email signature?

To ensure a positive and consistent brand experience enterprise-wide, do not include logo images in email signatures. Email signature instructions and recommended formatting can be found on the Brand Resource Center, located at HCAhealthcare.com/brand.

Can I request a custom logo or supporting logo for my group, division or department?

Approved lock-up logos for each group and division already exist and are available on the Brand Resource Center, located at HCAhealthcare.com/brand. For department or business units, write the name of your division or department in text.

Can I create my own logo for my team?

No. Do not use the Diamond Plus mark to create any new logo, icon or mark that appears separately from HCA Healthcare or without explicit permission from the Brand Team. Because we’re working hard to create consistency throughout all branding efforts in our footprint, it’s important to only use the approved logos and brand architecture.

Can I add a meeting or event title below the HCA Healthcare logo?

Please do not add any additional text below the HCA Healthcare logo as it may be construed as a unique logo. If additional context is needed, use the editable text fields within one of our brand templates to add to it. If you are creating materials for a large event that needs a custom logo, submit a Ask Brand request for Creative Services to review. The Brand Team must approve any and all logo requests.

What if I am using a legacy logo with the old HCA incorporated?

If you come across a logo that incorporates an old HCA design, please do not simply add the current HCA Healthcare logo in its place. This will not be compliant with our overall architecture. In this case, please contact Ask Brand.

Can I use lock-up logos on branded products or collateral?

Always default to using the HCA Healthcare logo on its own. If you have a specific use case when the HCA Healthcare logo would not meet your needs on certain collateral, please contact Ask Brand. While we can’t predict every nuance of our complex organization, we are committed to aligning our mission, messaging and visuals everywhere possible.

Can I place a logo on a photo?

When using the functional design style, the HCA Healthcare logo should not be placed over any photograph or image. If you are working on a project within the flexible or free design style, a single-color logo can be placed on a non-busy part of the photo.

My team still has assets with the old HCA Healthcare logo on them. Can I still use it?

Unfortunately, you cannot. Please make sure that all HCA Healthcare collateral features our correct logo and current brand look. You’ll find everything you need on the Brand Resource Center, located at HCAhealthcare.com/brand.

I work for both a division and a facility. Can I combine both logos on to one business card?

The HCA Healthcare logo is standard on all business cards. No other logos should replace the HCA Healthcare logo or be added to the business card. For individuals who support multiple areas, we suggest ordering more than one type of business card to use in different contexts.

Typography

Are the typefaces I use really that big of a deal?

Because we’re on a quest for brand consistency, absolutely. When using the functional and flexible design styles, please do everything in your power to use only Mark for HCA and Georgia Pro in printed materials. In the event you don’t have access to these fonts, your approved backups are Arial and Georgia.

What is the difference between a brand typeface and a system typeface?

A system typeface is a family of fonts distributed by Microsoft and/or Apple and installed on all computers that use Microsoft and/or Apple products. Our brand typeface is a family of fonts specifically chosen for use to tell the HCA Healthcare brand story.

Since our brand typeface has been custom-developed for HCA Healthcare, it may not be compatible with non-HCA Healthcare computers.

Color

Does it matter if I use a slightly different shade of the primary orange color?

There’s orange, and then there’s HCA Healthcare Orange. Use the brand specific color values for orange. The primary HCA Healthcare colors should not be used at a different shade; always use them at 100% tint. Please always be precise in your use of colors.

Maps

Where can I find the map I need? If the right one isn’t available, can I have one created?

A range of maps can be found on the Brand Resource Center library, located at HCAhealthcare.com/brand. If you can’t find the map you’re looking for, please contact Ask Brand.

Decorative Icons

When should I use which style of icon?

Use dual-color icons for larger, more elaborate pieces. Use single-color icons in smaller sizes for a more utilitarian look and in PowerPoint presentations. If you are working within the flexible or free design styles, you can work with Creative Services and the Brand Team to land on any approved additional icon usage.

Patterns

Can I redesign or alter any of the approved patterns?

For the sake of brand consistency, our patterns cannot be altered in the functional design style. Colors may be changed in the patterns permitted for flexible design style use, but they must maintain a monochromatic look and feel. Patterns in the free design style may be altered in color or element positioning. Note that all patterns should be scaled proportionally when scaling larger or smaller as needed.

Additionally, our design elements work within either the functional, flexible or free design style. Design needs that fall within the functional design style use only our core elements, colors, patterns and typography. The flexible design style allows for additional colors, patterns and/or layout options. This style requires a trained designer. If your team does not have a trained designer, you will need to submit a Ask Brand request for any collateral you wish to design using the flexible design style. Lastly, the free design style only applies to projects that have received Brand Team approval, and are usually one-off campaigns.

Photography

How do I access our library of approved photography?

You’ll find all approved photography hosted on the Brand Resource Center, located at HCAhealthcare.com/brand.

Can I use photography that’s not found in our library?

We update our asset repositories on a quarterly basis. In the meantime, if you are in need of a photo that is not in our repository, please visit gettyimages.com and note the creative number assigned to the photo you select. You can then submit an Ask Brand request to have the photo downloaded for use.

How do I access our library of approved photography?

You’ll find all approved photography hosted on MediaWorks. If you have questions, please contact Creative Services.

Can I manipulate photography in any way?

For legal and business reasons, you may only crop photos. Please do not alter the color or composition of the approved photography found on the Brand Resource Center, located at HCAhealthcare.com/brand.

Design Applications

May I use previous/old resources, such as letterhead, for professional communications on behalf of HCA Healthcare?

No. Please be sure to communicate on behalf of HCA Healthcare with approved resources from our current toolkit, found on the Brand Resource Center, located at HCAhealthcare.com/brand. Materials that were created within Brand Book 1.0 and 1.5 guidelines are still compliant.

What is the difference between a fullbleed and non-bleed asset?

Full-bleed assets are the ones in which color runs all the way to the edge of the page. A full-bleed asset can only be ordered through an approved vendor, while a nonbleed asset can be either ordered from an approved vendor or printed in the office as needed.

May I print my own assets, such as postcards?

It depends on which asset you need. Postcards are full-bleed templates, which means they must be printed by an approved vendor. However, letterhead offers a nonbleed version that you can print yourself.

How do I add an email signature to all of my Outlook emails?

Please follow the step-by-step instructions available on the Brand Resource Center, located at HCAhealthcare.com/brand.

Does it matter which PowerPoint template I use?

You can choose which title and breaker slide you use. This should allow you to personalize your presentation and help keep the many presentations we create from looking identical. However, please adhere to our master templates, which are available in standard and widescreen formats.

Can I continue using the old PowerPoint format?

No. In order to ensure brand consistency, we must use the same master template. While we can appreciate your comfort with the previous templates, it’s important our presentation formats match our branding elements.

Where can I access the new PowerPoint templates?

The PowerPoint templates, along with other branded templates, are available on the Brand Resource Center, located at HCAhealthcare.com/brand.

How do I decide which template option to use for posters, flyers, postcards, etc.?

Consider your audience, objective and content. If your material is information-dense, you may want to choose an option with photography to break up long portions of text. If it’s a brief message of crucial importance, consider leveraging a solid HCA Healthcare Orange layout.
