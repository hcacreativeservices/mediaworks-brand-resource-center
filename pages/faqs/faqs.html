FAQs

The Why and The How

Why did we build a new, purpose-driven brand?

The fact is, HCA Healthcare is doing incredible things. The new branding will help us uniformly connect to and convey our purpose and our story – how we show up for work each day, what we collectively stand for, and, importantly, how we want patients to feel when they set foot in one of our facilities.

How is this different from what we have been doing?

This is a change in the way we communicate. It is how we can be consistent across the organization, to current colleagues and candidates, about what makes HCA Healthcare unique and about our opportunities based on our scale. We are bigger and more complex than when we were founded in 1968, and this will help us better engage colleagues and candidates, to attract them and reinforce the opportunities they have working here.

Isn’t a brand just a logo?

Our brand is much more than just a logo. Our brand is made up of our combined efforts, interactions, and communications. Our new logo, fonts, colors, visual elements, word choices and messaging all work together to help us represent and communicate about our brand.

How will this affect our facilities?

We are not going to brand every facility as HCA Healthcare because we have great local brands in many communities already. But in areas where we have no brand to tie our access points together, this limits our ability to market ourselves as a system solution for patients, nurses and physicians. This is especially important to private insurers as they build their networks.

So, we’re exploring the use of HCA Healthcare as the umbrella brand in some of our communities where there is no market brand. That work has started in Houston and Florida, and subsequent areas will be addressed in the coming years via a phased approach.

But we want to be clear: defining and evolving our market brands is going to take considerable coordination among our teams. It’s a multi-phase effort that will have both short-term and long-term goals, so our approach is one of close coordination to ensure that we are all in sync moving along the same path.

Will this foster greater collaboration across our organization?

Yes. When we unite under one purpose, we can collaborate to raise the bar in healthcare. Care delivered in one location can change care delivered everywhere.

How will this affect my day-to-day?

For many of you, it won’t change a thing. That fact is, it doesn’t matter whether one of our facilities carries the HCA Healthcare logo. We’re all part of the same family, committed to providing the highest quality care our patients need and deserve.

I don’t work directly with patients in my job. Is the brand relevant for me too?

Yes, this is for everyone in every job within HCA Healthcare. No matter what you do, our brand purpose and promise connect all of us as one organization.

Our Facilities

Will we remodel facilities to look the same?

No, we have no plans to make physical renovations to standardize the look of our facilities.

Will this change the local personality of my facility?

We know it is important for each facility to reflect and serve their communities with their unique personalities. That will not change. This is about uncovering HCA Healthcare’s value and what we have to offer.

Will we reassign colleagues to different facilities as part of this?

The brand initiative does not impact staffing.

Brand Governance/Brand Stewardship

What is brand governance?

Brand governance is a formalized review process through which we can ensure consistent design and messaging across the enterprise.

What is different about HCA Healthcare’s approach to brand governance?

We are not the “logo police.” Our governance model is a stewardship model where our hope is to educate, support and empower colleagues as we all work towards being good stewards of the brand.

What is brand stewardship?

Stewardship is the activity or job of being responsible for something. In our case, that something is our brand. Through brand stewardship,our hope is to educate, support and empower colleagues as we all work towards being good stewards of the brand.

Why should we all be brand stewards?

To ensure that the new HCA Healthcare brand look and feel is incorporated and used correctly to protect our legacy, culture and mission, we must all take ownership of our new brand.

How can I be a good brand steward?

Educate yourself on our new brand. Understand the why. Read through the guidelines of how to put our new brand to work. Ensure that you are utilizing our online toolkit for all your asset needs. Finally, make sure that you are helping to direct colleagues to the story behind our new brand and our easy-to-use resources.

Logo

May I use the old HCA Healthcare logo?

No. On all communications, only the current HCA Healthcare logo may be used.

Can I create my own logo for my team?

No. Since we’re working hard to create consistency throughout all branding efforts in our footprint, it’s important to use the approved logos and brand architecture.

What’s the process for requesting a logo?

First, check to see if the logo you need is on MediaWorks. If the logo does not already exist, you may request a custom logo by visiting LOCATION. To move your request through the system quickly and accurately, please know the background to your request. Some questions to keep in mind are: Is this an ongoing initiative? Will the logo be used internally or externally? Does a legacy logo exist, or is this a request for a new logo?

My team uses a legacy internal logo. Does it need to be redesigned or approved?

Yes. Please request an updated HCA Healthcare internal logo by visiting LOCATION.

Colors/Fonts

Can I use a slightly different shade of the primary orange color?

No. There’s orange—and then there’s HCA Healthcare Orange. More specifically, Pantone 7579 C. Please be precise in your use of colors.

Are the typefaces I use really that big of a deal?

Because we’re on a quest for brand consistency, absolutely. Please do everything in your power to use only Mark for HCA and Georgia Pro. In the event you don’t have access to these fonts, your approved backups are Arial and Georgia.

Can I adapt any of the icon colors for my project?

Yes, but only a single-color icon’s color can change. When updating a single-color icon, make sure its color matches the primary color in the asset. (For example, an asset with a green headline font should have a matching green, single-color icon.)

How should I choose between a single-color icon and a dual-tone icon?

Use dual-color icons for larger, more elaborate pieces. Use single-color icons in smaller sizes for a more utilitarian look.

Patterns/Maps

Can I redesign any of the approved patterns?

Our patterns add a recognizable visual element to our corporate communications, so for the sake of brand consistency, please don’t alter them. However, you can resize the pattern to be larger or smaller as needed.

Where can I find the map I need? If the right one isn’t available, can I have one created?

A range of maps can be found in the MediaWorks library. If you can’t find the map you’re looking for, please contact Creative Services to create one that works specifically for your project.

Powerpoint

Does it matter which PowerPoint template I use?

There is some flexibility in which title and breaker slides you use. This should allow you to personalize your presentation and help keep the many presentations that we do from looking identical. However, there is only one master template that must be adhered to.

Can I continue using the old PowerPoint format?

No. To usher in our new brand consistently, we must leverage the new template. We can appreciate your comfort with the templates you’ve used up until now. But it’s important that our presentation formats match our new branding elements.

Where can I access the new PowerPoint templates?

The HCA Healthcare PowerPoint template should address all your presentation needs, and is available here.

Communication and Messaging

Can I ever use “HCA” instead of “HCA Healthcare?”

To be consistent in how we talk about the organization, we are always HCA Healthcare. Not “HCA,” nor subsequent versions such as “Hospital Corporation of America.” And as you can read from our Lexicon Guide, “Healthcare” is always one word, and always capitalized in the usage of our name. It may be hard to get used to at first, but when we speak in a unified way about HCA Healthcare, we better maintain our brand identity.

How do I learn more about AP style?

The Associated Press Stylebook is a writing style guide that outlines rules on grammar, punctuation, usage and journalistic style. You can access the stylebook in print or online at apstylebook.com.

Toolkit

May I use my own toolkit resources, such as letterhead, for professional communications on behalf of HCA Healthcare?

No. Please be sure to communicate on behalf of HCA Healthcare with approved resources from our new toolkit.

My team still has a folder with the old HCA Healthcare logo on it. Can I use it?

Unfortunately, you cannot. Please make sure that all communications on behalf of HCA Healthcare feature our new logo and brand look. You’ll find everything you need in our toolkit.

May I print my own toolkit assets, such as postcards?

It depends on which toolkit asset you need. Postcards are full-bleed templates that must be printed by an approved vendor. But letterhead offers a non-bleed version that you can print yourself.

What is the difference between a full-bleed and non-bleed toolkit asset?

Full-bleed assets are the ones in which color runs all the way to the edge of the page. A full-bleed asset can be ordered only through an approved vendor, while a non-bleed asset can be ordered from an approved vendor or printed in the office, as needed.

How do I decide which template option to use for posters, flyers, postcards, etc.?

Consider your audience, objective and content. If it’s information-dense, you may want to choose an option with photography to break up long portions of text. If it’s a brief message of crucial importance, consider leveraging a solid HCA Healthcare Orange layout.

How do I add an email signature to all my Outlook emails?

Please follow the step-by-step instructions available on MediaWorks. If you have questions, please contact Creative Services.

Do all corporate communications pieces require a CTA and end matter?

Yes, every piece of corporate communication features unique end matter and a call-to-action. Please ensure that the asset you’re creating follows the guidelines. If you have additional questions, please email brand@HCAhealthcare.com.

Photography

Can I use photography that’s not found in our library?

No. For legal and business reasons, you must use only photography found on MediaWorks.

How do I access our library of approved photography?

You’ll find all approved photography hosted on MediaWorks. If you have questions, please contact Creative Services.

Can I place a logo on a photo?

No. At this time, please do not place the HCA Healthcare logo over any photograph or image.

How can I place an order with the HCA Healthcare Company Store?
