Language do's and don'ts

This table will provide guidelines for how to talk or write about HCA Healthcare. Many of these are based on AP style guidelines, though we have provided our own nuances to reflect who we are and how we communicate on the AP style rules and exceptions page.

Do Don't
Use our full name — HCA Healthcare — when talking about our organization. Don’t use a name other than HCA Healthcare.
Keep HCA Healthcare on the same line within text. Don’t break HCA and Healthcare with a line break.
Spell healthcare as one word with no space. Don’t spell healthcare as health care.
Spell out abbreviations on first reference. Don’t introduce an abbreviation without first spelling out the phrase it stands for.
Use sentence case in titles, except in presentations and publications where initial caps are appropriate for title slides, breaker slides and title pages. Don’t use all caps in titles, and in general limit the use of caps in text as they can cause issues with AA accessibility.
Capitalize names of our divisions, markets, departments, groups, awards and programs. Don’t capitalize division, department, award or program unless they are part of a proper name.
Capitalize job titles/job roles only under signatures, email signatures or on business cards. Don’t capitalize job titles/job roles in text except when they precede the person’s name.
Use the word and instead of the ampersand symbol. Don’t use & except in department names, for example Marketing & Corporate Affairs.
Use punctuation if bulleted points are complete sentences — and within a bulleted list, do be consistent with the grammatical structure of each bulleted item. Don’t use punctuation if bulleted points are fragments, and don’t switch between the two in one bulleted list — be consistent.
When using an em dash, include spaces between the dash and the words surrounding it. Don't use an em dash without spaces between it and the surrounding words; don't use a hyphen in place of a dash.
Talk to your audience. Don’t talk about your audience.
Use friendly, natural, conversational language, as if you’re talking to a friend. Don’t use stilted, stuffy or overly corporate language.
Use understandable words and phrases. Don’t use corporate or industry jargon.
Write in the active voice. Don’t write in the passive voice.
Back up your claims with facts, and always include the date of each fact within the text or as a footnote. Don’t use absolutes, hyperbole or superlatives unless we have the evidence to back it up.
Say definitively what we do. Don’t use maybe or might — we say what we do and we do what we say.
Reference our mission statement in its entirety. Don’t use any part of our mission statement, Above all else, we are committed to the care and improvement of human life, out of context.
Use designated HCA Healthcare hashtags to allow colleagues, patients and consumers to index and easily find content relevant to our brand. Examples of approved enterprise hashtags can be found on the Phrases to use page.
Refer to the AP Stylebook if you have questions about punctuation or grammar that are not covered here.
