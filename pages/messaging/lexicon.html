About our lexicon

Why do we need a lexicon?

Our word choices and writing style convey a message about who we are, and we want to ensure that we speak with a unified, consistent voice. It’s why we’ve put together a lexicon and guidelines: the clearer and more consistent we can all be, the easier it will be for our audiences to know who we are and what we stand for.

Word choice

In our lexicon you’ll find certain words that we prefer you use over others, suggested phrases that can convey our purpose and identity, and the do’s and don’ts for communicating about HCA Healthcare.

We’ve also included guidance on how to communicate about extended brands while staying true to the HCA Healthcare brand.

Voice

We try to use simple language and write in a friendly, natural, conversational tone. We talk to our audience, not about them.

Style

For the foundational rules of grammar and punctuation, we follow AP Style guidelines–with one major exception (see sidebar). In situations where we haven’t provided specific direction in this Brand Book, please refer to the AP Stylebook for guidance.

Using our name

To be consistent in how we talk about ourselves, we are always HCA Healthcare: not HCA, Hospital Corporation of America, HCAH or any other name, even after first reference. Healthcare is always one word and always capitalized in the usage of our name.

HCAhealthcare.com is our URL style. Always set HCA in all caps and lowercase healthcare.com. This gives equal weight to both words in our name, and helps to reinforce recognition of our full brand name.

When we speak in a unified way about HCA Healthcare, we better maintain our brand identity.

Healthcare

One major instance where we don’t follow AP Style guidelines is with the word healthcare. (Healthcare should be italicized here and in the second sentence.)
