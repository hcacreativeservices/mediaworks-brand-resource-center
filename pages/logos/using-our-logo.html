I’ve seen it. Now how do I use it?

The HCA Healthcare logo is the cornerstone of our visual identity. It must appear on every piece of our communication, as these standards state. Consistent presentation of the logo is crucial across all media.

The logo is not to be altered or reconstructed using an unapproved typeface. The typeface that makes up our logo was carefully refined to be owned by our brand.

Minimum Size

The logo should always be at least 0.5 inches wide in print or 70 pixels wide when used online. When using the smallest approved sizes consider removing the service mark.

Grayscale Usage

Our logo may be represented in solid black within a grayscale (black and white) application. It can also appear as white type in its grayscale form when placed atop a solid black background.

Color Application

Our logo may be placed on one of two colors at 100% strength, using either HCA Healthcare Orange or HCA Healthcare Navy. When placed on HCA Healthcare Orange the entire logo should be white, as shown. When placed on HCA Healthcare Navy, “HCA” and “Healthcare” should be white and the Diamond Plus should be HCA Healthcare Orange.

The wonder of space

The HCA Healthcare logo must always be surrounded by clear space that contains neither text nor graphics. The distance surrounding the logo should be consistent with the height or width of the Diamond Plus mark.
